Home-made RC Car April 2015

Project Description

As a final project for a high school computer science class, I built a remote controlled car in the span of 4 hours. The mechanical components of the car are comprised completely of regular household items. To control the car, I configured an on board arduino to receive communications through a bluetooth module paired with a smartphone.

Project Details

  • The frame of the car consists of wooden barbeque skewers bound together in masking tape. Each wheel is a pair of gatorade bottle caps taped together and pierced through the centre
  • The two motors were taken from old/broken toys. The arduino could not provide enough current to power them. Instead, I borrowed 2 relays from the school shop so that the motors could be powered through a more sufficient Lithium Polymer battery.




Unfortunately, the two motors I was able to find were not identical, and ran at different speeds when connected to the same power source. I was able to remedy this to some extent by using PWM signals from the arduino to run the faster motor for only a fraction of the time. However, even this solution wasnt optimal -- due to the time required for the switch of the mechanical relay, the on/off signals could not be altered at a high frequency; this lead to the car driving in a zig zag pattern to go straight. If I make edits to the car, I will likely find a purely electronic relay, such as a MOSFET, to use instead.
